When your brain gets stuck
🔁 Feeling stuck on something?
It happens. Your brain latches onto something and it's hard to let it go. That's okay — here's what to try.
1
Name it and park it
Say to yourself: "I'm thinking about [thing] again." Just noticing it is the first step. You don't have to stop thinking about it — just notice that it's happening.
○
2
Check the schedule
If it's about leaving time, the park, or what's happening next — the answer is already on the Schedule tab. Check there first before asking anyone.
○
3
Give yourself a time limit
Tell yourself: "I'll think about this for 30 more seconds, then I'm switching to something else." Set the timer on your phone if it helps. When it's done, it's done for now.
○
4
Switch your focus deliberately
Look at the Topic of the Day on the Schedule tab. Try thinking about that instead — even for a minute. Your brain can only really focus on one thing at a time.
○
5
Do something with your hands or body
Move around, use a fidget, press your feet into the floor, take a slow breath. Getting out of your head and into your body helps break the loop.
○
6
It will come back around
Whatever you're thinking about isn't going anywhere. You can come back to it later — at break, at lunch, or after school. Right now isn't the time, but later is.
